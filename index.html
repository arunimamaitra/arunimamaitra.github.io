Arunima Maitra

I am a Research Assistant at the Connected Informatics and Built Environment Research (CIBER) Lab in the Institute of Behavioral Science at the University of Colorado Boulder. Previously, I completed my M.S. in Computer Science at the University of Colorado Boulder, where I was advised by Katharina von der Wense.

Research Interests

My research focuses on Large Language Models, Human-Computer Interaction, and Computational Social Science. I am currently interested in the following research directions:

  1. Human-AI Interaction: Understanding and improving how humans collaborate and communicate with large language models.
  2. Language and Reasoning in AI: Analyzing how language generated by models differs from human communication in structure, reasoning, and conversational norms.
  3. Computational Social Science: Using language models and data-driven methods to study social behavior, attitudes, and polarization.
